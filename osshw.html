Sound devices supported by Open Sound System

At this moment Open Sound System (OSS) supports the following sound cards and sound chipsets.

This list is not 100% complete. There are dozens of sound cards that are based on some standard sound chips (or motherboard chipset) made by vendors like Intel, VIA, Cirrus/Crystal, Analog Devices, Realtek, Yamaha, C'Media, Trident, Sigmatel and many others. Such cards may not be listed in the following list but they are still supported. Just look at the list of "Generic ..." devices below.

IN PARTICULAR ALL AC97 SOUND DEVICES ARE SUPPORTED BY OSS EVEN THEY ARE NOT LISTED HERE. ALSO ALL MOTHERBOARD CHIPSETS ARE SUPPORTED OR AT LEAST WILL BE SUPPORTED WITHIN FEW WEEKS FROM THEIR ANNOUNCEMENT.

Please note that this list is valid for Linux. Some of the drivers are not available under the other operating systems. The sound card lists for the "PC" operating systems like FreeBSD, NetBSD, OpenBSD, Solaris/x86, LynxOS and SCO OpenServer are fairly complete. However only a limited set of PCI devices are supported under RISC workstation architectures like AIX, Tru64, HPUX and Solaris-Sparc. AIX supports all ISA/PnP devices. Please contact sales@opensound.com if you are unsure if your system/sound card combination is supported.

Some of the devices below may be listed as "NOT SUPPORTED" or "NOT SUPPORTED YET". This means that we do not have any drivers available for such devices yet. They may be under development but we cannot give any release dates.

"BETA" means that the driver is available but that some of the features are still missing from the driver. However in general there are no reliability problems in the drivers we release as beta ones. If there are any known problems they will be listed in the README file for the device.
